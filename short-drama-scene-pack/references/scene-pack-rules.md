# Scene Pack Rules

Use this file when breaking a script into shootable scene units.

## Prefer These

- one dramatic purpose per scene
- visible actions instead of emotional summaries
- controlled locations
- clear handoff hook into the next beat
- one key image the production team can remember

## Avoid These

- rewriting the script in paragraph form
- abstract labels with no screen action
- over-splitting for no reason
- under-splitting scenes with two different emotional jobs
- production notes that secretly alter suspense timing

## Good Scene Purpose Examples

- she confirms the phone contains hidden-recording material
- the mother sees the evidence and chooses silence
- the danger moves from suspicion to family cover-up

## Good Key Image Examples

- phone screen flashing a hallway hidden-recording screenshot
- mother holding the phone with whitening knuckles
- smiling uncle outside the locked bedroom door

## Good Continuity Locks

- keep the phone as the central proof object
- preserve who has seen the evidence by the end of the episode
- do not visually reveal more than the script has earned
- keep the episode's final question open

## Good Episode-End Snapshot Fields

- who knows what
- proof status
- emotional pressure still active
- relationship shift
- next-episode carry question
