# Negative Rules

Use this file to stop scene packs from becoming vague or continuity-breaking.

## 1. Do Not Rewrite the Story

Reject:

- new scenes that alter causality
- moved reveals that reduce suspense
- added confrontations that do not exist in the script
- cleaner logic at the cost of future episode setup

## 2. Do Not Output Empty Production Language

Reject:

- intense atmosphere
- strong tension
- emotional collision
- dramatic pressure

on their own.

Require:

- who does what
- what is seen on screen
- what changes by the end of the scene

## 3. Do Not Lose the Hook

Reject scene cards that end blandly.

Weak:

- scene ends with uncertainty

Strong:

- mother lowers her voice and says, "You do not tell anyone yet."
- the uncle smiles outside the bedroom door while asking for his phone back

## 4. Do Not Blur Scenes Together

Reject:

- one long summary covering multiple locations
- scene cards with the same purpose repeated three times
- emotional beats merged so tightly that storyboard work becomes guesswork

## 5. Do Not Invent Visual Canon

Reject:

- camera style rules unsupported by the request
- prop details unsupported by the script
- exact costume details unsupported by the character pack

If needed, mark details as missing instead of inventing them.

## 6. Do Not Break Downstream Use

Reject packs that are too vague for:

- image prompt generation
- storyboard generation
- directing

Every scene should leave the next stage knowing:

- where we are
- who is there
- what the shot must communicate
- what must be preserved for continuity
