# Short Drama Second Review

Review an existing short-drama script like a ruthless second-pass editor.

This skill is for script-level commercial review, not storyboard assembly review.

## Main Goal

Judge whether the script or outline is commercially sharp enough for short-drama release.

Focus on:

- opening 3 seconds
- episode-end hook
- pain point
- payoff point
- reversal freshness
- click-next potential

## Hard Boundary

Do not change the storyline.

Do not:

- replace the premise
- rewrite the core plot path
- change who did what
- change character relationships
- change cause-and-effect logic
- add a twist that breaks later continuity

## Not My Job

- I do not review assembled storyboard production handoff quality.
- I do not judge panel-level hook placement.
- I do not judge prop drift, room drift, or image-generation continuity risk.
- I do not produce final operator handoff notes.

## Review Questions

Focus on:

1. Is the first 3 seconds strong enough
2. Is the episode-end hook strong enough
3. Are pain point, payoff point, and reversal too ordinary
4. Is the hook special or too standard
5. Is the story commercially sharp enough for short-video drama

## Output Rule

Stay at script / outline / episode-draft level.

Do not drift into:

- storyboard prompt review
- assembled board pacing review
- production continuity risk summary
- operator handoff readiness review
