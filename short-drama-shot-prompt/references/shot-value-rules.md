# Shot Value Rules

Use these rules when a prompt is accurate but not attractive enough.

## Core Principle

The prompt should not only describe the panel.

It should preserve what makes the panel worth generating.

## What The Prompt Must Protect

- the first-look center
- the commercial payload
- the most legible proof, charge, pressure, or reversal

## High-Value Prompt Priorities

### Proof

Make the evidence visually legible.

Prefer:

- exact arrangement
- exact surface
- exact visible grouping

Avoid:

- vague "looking at phone"
- explaining what the proof means

### Reaction

Keep trigger and body state in the same frame logic.

Prefer:

- face plus object
- eyes plus screen
- frozen hand still in contact with the trigger

Avoid:

- generic shocked pose
- pure emotional label with no visible cause

### Chemistry

Protect the relation between bodies.

Prefer:

- distance
- gaze
- asymmetry
- interruption
- blocked touch

Avoid:

- beauty wording with no tension
- balanced posing that kills charge

### Humiliation And Confrontation

Protect the visible power relation.

Prefer:

- who occupies the center
- who is exposed
- who is boxed in
- who is physically above, closer, or backed by others

Avoid:

- coverage-style wording
- neutral staging

## Compression Rule

Keep only what sharpens:

- readability
- emotional hit
- hook value

Cut anything decorative that weakens the main read.
