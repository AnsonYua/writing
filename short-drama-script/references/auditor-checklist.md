# Auditor Checklist

Use this checklist when the draft feels weak, generic, or over-engineered.

## Premise Justification

- What is the first thing a skeptical viewer would say does not make sense?
- Does the setup sound natural when spoken out loud?
- Is the story using the right real-world term, or a dramatic term that sounds wrong?
- Does the twist create emotion first, or confusion first?
- Is the supposed reversal actually a reversal, or only more bad news?
- If a key setup needs too much explanation, can it be simplified?
- Is the script trying to protect a weak premise instead of replacing it with a stronger one?
- If the problem is foundational, should the story restart from premise instead of patching scenes?

## Viewer Reaction

- As a viewer, what would annoy me first?
- As a viewer, where would I feel pain for the protagonist?
- Is that pain specific enough, or too vague?
- Do I want revenge, justice, dignity, money, love, or truth for the protagonist?
- Does the script clearly tell me what emotional payoff I should wait for?
- Would I comment "so good", "so painful", "hurry up and hit back", or would I just shrug?
- Does this feel special, or like a standard-answer comeback story?
- Is the most memorable angle actually memorable?
- After hearing the hook once, can I already predict the whole story too easily?
- Is "he was underestimated" the only thing carrying the hook?
- If this is not special enough, what angle should be rebuilt from the beginning?

## Hook

- Does the first beat create immediate curiosity, danger, humiliation, or loss?
- Can the audience understand the core situation within seconds?
- Is the hook a real reversal, or only escalation?

## Conflict

- Does the protagonist want something concrete right now?
- Is someone actively blocking that desire?
- Does the scene get worse, not just longer?

## Dialogue

- Can each line be spoken naturally?
- Are any lines explaining what the viewer can already infer?
- Do the main characters sound different from each other?

## De-AI

- Remove summary phrases that label emotions instead of showing them
- Remove therapy-speak, essay-speak, and moral commentary
- Replace repeated sentence rhythm with sharper variation

## Cliffhanger

- Does the final beat force a next-click?
- Would a viewer comment or argue about what happens next?
