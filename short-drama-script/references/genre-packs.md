# Genre Packs

Apply the closest pack when the user's premise clearly fits one.

## Sweet Romance

Use for:

- contract love
- hidden crush
- reunion romance
- soft-pull emotional tension

Principles:

- restraint beats over-explaining
- emotional push-pull matters more than plot complexity
- small reactions beat long confessions

Avoid:

- melodrama so heavy it breaks tenderness
- everyone speaking in the same poetic voice

## Urban Workplace

Use for:

- office conflict
- power imbalance
- promotion battles
- public humiliation at work

Principles:

- realism is persuasive
- conflict lives in status, language, timing, and leverage
- growth should cost something

Avoid:

- magic fixes
- unrealistic authority jumps
- empty strategy talk instead of visible action

Language rule:

- show judgment through what the character does with people, money, pressure, and information

## Revenge Melodrama

Use for:

- betrayal
- public humiliation
- comeback
- family or class revenge

Principles:

- suppress hard before release
- every payoff should be visible and social
- humiliation and reversal should be clean and easy to feel

Avoid:

- revenge that lands only in internal monologue
- weak antagonists who fold too early

## School / Youth Drama

Use for:

- campus humiliation
- secret dating
- class gap
- popularity hierarchies

Principles:

- emotions should feel immediate and simple to read
- status shifts should happen in public spaces
- friendships and betrayals should change fast

Avoid:

- adult-sounding dialogue
- over-complicated backstory in Episode 1

## Suspense Romance

Use for:

- hidden identity
- dangerous attraction
- lies, surveillance, disappearance, blackmail

Principles:

- build with information asymmetry
- romance should increase danger, not pause it
- clues should be active, not lecture-like

Avoid:

- exposition dumps
- twists with no emotional cost
