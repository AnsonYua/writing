# Negative Rules

Use this file as a hard filter before presenting a script draft.

## 1. Fake Reversal

Reject these:

- bad news followed by worse news
- humiliation followed by more humiliation
- "your life is over, and actually it is even more over"
- a dramatic sentence that does not change audience understanding

Require this instead:

- the viewer realizes they understood the situation wrongly
- power changes hands in the moment
- the protagonist gains leverage, information, timing, witness, or proof
- the apparent loss opens a new weapon or path

## 2. Forced Twist

Reject these unless strongly set up:

- bankrupt company but somehow glorious inheritance
- sudden rich-parent reveal with no prior trace
- genius AI author reveal with no believable planting
- one phone call that solves the whole world
- legal, corporate, or family mechanisms that exist only to force a twist

Ask:

- would a normal viewer say "why would it work like that?"
- is the twist emotionally strong because it is true to the setup, or only because it is loud?

## 3. Generic Comeback

Reject these:

- standard office slap-back with no distinctive story engine
- boss mocks protagonist, system/project/result immediately proves him right in a routine way
- "they all looked down on him" without a fresh relationship or cost structure

Require at least one:

- unusual social pressure
- distinctive wound
- non-standard arena
- uncommon emotional contradiction
- consequence that hits family, dignity, money, or safety in a sharper way

## 4. Weak Pain

Reject pain that stays abstract.

Weak:

- he feels sad
- she is under pressure
- life is hard lately

Strong:

- mortgage due in three days
- child tuition unpaid
- parent needs surgery money
- fiancee publicly dumps him
- everyone sees him lose face
- family asks him to stay silent to keep peace

## 5. Repetitive Hooking

Do not let every episode-end hook be the same type.

Vary between:

- proof reveal
- identity crack
- betrayal reveal
- moral trap
- countdown
- wrong accusation
- secret witness
- emotional choice

## 6. AI-Like Writing

Reject these:

- emotion labels instead of behavior
- lecture lines
- symmetrical paragraph rhythm
- repeated sentence openings
- polished but bloodless dialogue
- characters who all speak with the same sharpness

Prefer:

- interruption
- gesture
- silence
- subtext
- incomplete speech under pressure

## 7. Logic Softness

Reject these:

- a problem exists only because everyone refuses normal communication for no reason
- a scene works only if side characters lose common sense
- the antagonist is evil but has no believable incentive or social cover
- the protagonist discovers key truth too conveniently

Fix by:

- adding pressure, time limit, status gap, or fear
- making access to proof difficult but not impossible
- giving each side a believable reason to act that way
