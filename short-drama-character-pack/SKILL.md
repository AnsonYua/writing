# Short Drama Character Pack

Extract a reusable character pack from an existing short-drama script.

This skill is for locking character consistency after the story draft already exists.

Read [negative-rules.md](C:\Users\anson\Desktop\writing\out\short-drama-character-pack\references\negative-rules.md) before finalizing character cards.

## Hard Boundary

Do not change the storyline.

That means:

- do not rewrite plot
- do not change character relationships
- do not invent major new backstory
- do not add twists
- do not alter who knows what

If the script does not provide enough detail, infer cautiously and label it as:

- inferred from context

Do not present guesses as hard canon unless the script already supports them.

## Main Goal

Turn story material into a stable character bible that can be reused for:

- image generation
- scene-pack creation
- storyboard generation
- continuity checking
- future episode consistency

## What To Extract

For each important character, extract:

1. Identity
2. Story function
3. Relationship position
4. Core appearance
5. Clothing system
6. Visual anchors
7. Emotional and behavioral habits
8. Voice and speaking feel
9. Continuity locks
10. Short prompt-ready version

## Extraction Rules

- prefer details explicitly shown in the script
- prefer repeatable visual features over decorative detail
- choose 2-4 memorable anchors, not 20 vague traits
- lock what must stay stable across episodes
- keep output useful for downstream production, not literary analysis

## Visual Anchor Rule

Every major character must have at least one clear visual anchor.

Examples:

- always wears a faded office shirt
- right wrist black cord
- cheap shoulder bag with cracked strap
- hair always tied tight
- eyes tired but watchful

Anchors should be easy to remember and easy to keep consistent.

## Continuity Lock Rule

Each major character must have "do not drift" notes.

Examples:

- do not make her look older than a high-school student
- do not turn a restrained mother into an openly aggressive character without story cause
- do not make the uncle look comic or exaggerated if the story needs normal-face danger

## Output Format

Use this structure:

### Character Pack Summary

- story title
- source material used
- major characters extracted
- any major missing details

### Character Cards

For each major character, output:

#### Name

- role in story
- age or age band
- relationship map
- story function
- explicit details from script
- inferred details from context
- core appearance
- clothing system
- visual anchors
- behavior and emotional habits
- speaking feel
- continuity locks
- quick prompt version

### Casting Priority

Label each character as:

- primary
- secondary
- support

### Style Notes For Downstream Use

Brief notes for image or storyboard stages:

- which characters must stay visually strongest
- which characters must never be confused with each other
- which details are essential vs optional

## Tone

Be practical and concrete.

Do not overwrite the writer's story with your own imagination.
Do not pad with generic beauty-description language.
Make the pack easy to reuse.

## Negative Rule Check

Fail the pack and rewrite the affected card if it does any of these:

- makes different characters feel visually interchangeable
- invents unsupported backstory as if it were canon
- uses generic beauty words instead of stable visual anchors
- changes age band, class feel, hairstyle logic, or body frame without support
- turns every strong character into the same "cold/sharp" type
- softens a dangerous character into a comic type when the story needs normal-face threat
- sexualizes minors or gives age-inappropriate styling
- ignores the exact details needed for later continuity
