# Negative Rules

Use this file to stop character-pack drift.

## 1. Do Not Invent Canon

Reject:

- made-up childhood trauma
- made-up job history
- made-up family status
- made-up personality labels not grounded in scenes

If detail is missing:

- infer cautiously
- label it as inferred from context

## 2. Do Not Use Generic Beauty Language

Reject:

- beautiful
- handsome
- elegant
- cold
- sharp
- noble

on their own.

Require concrete anchors:

- face shape
- posture
- hairstyle
- skin condition
- clothing wear pattern
- accessories
- gait
- eye state

## 3. Do Not Let Characters Blur Together

Reject packs where two major characters share the same:

- silhouette
- social aura
- default facial energy
- clothing logic
- speaking feel

unless the script clearly demands resemblance.

## 4. Do Not Drift on Age or Social Position

Lock:

- age band
- class signal
- grooming standard
- body condition
- workwear/homewear logic

Do not quietly upgrade a tired office worker into a polished idol look.
Do not make a protective mother look like a glamour antagonist unless the script says so.

## 5. Do Not Soften Threat by Accident

For danger roles, reject:

- cartoon-villain styling
- exaggerated creepy styling without story basis
- comic relief face language

Prefer normal-face danger:

- looks ordinary enough to exist inside family or workplace trust
- threat comes from behavior, access, and calmness

## 6. Minor Safety

Reject:

- sexualized styling for minors
- adult-coded makeup or body framing without textual support
- glamour language applied to underage characters

Keep age-appropriate, protective, and production-safe.

## 7. Downstream Production Use

Reject cards that are too vague for later image or storyboard work.

Every major card should make it easy to keep stable:

- hair
- clothing logic
- body impression
- energy
- one to three anchors
